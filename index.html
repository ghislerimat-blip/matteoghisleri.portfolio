I tell stories at the margins — of communities, of language, of morality. Cinema as a necessary act.

Matteo Ghisleri is a Turin-based director and screenwriter. He has been writing since 2019 and directing since 2020. His first short film, Aut Aut (2021), marks the beginning of a cinematic research that moves through the surreal, social marginality, and Italy as a moral landscape. His latest short, QUI, is currently in final post-production, produced by Panoramic Studio.

He works as a script editor, accompanying other directors' projects through development. He is currently editing a debut feature film in development for the American market.

In 2024 he co-founded, with four other Turin-based directors, a shared platform — now evolving into a fully-fledged collective for production, research and distribution.

Short Film · In Post-Production

QUI
Gigi is 14. Every day he bikes through the Vercelli flatlands delivering sandwiches and beer to tractor drivers in the rice fields. One afternoon he faints on a bridge and wakes up at Ruga Rotta — a wasteland on the edge of the countryside, ruled by bikers, armed children, a gypsy violinist and an elegant man who spins a roulette wheel. Gigi doesn't understand. He watches. Then he goes back to the bridge.
